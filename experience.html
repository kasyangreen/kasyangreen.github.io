Class Project | Time Series Analysis of Mangrove in Myanmar


Spring 2021


This project used Google Earth Engine to map Mangrove deforestation in Myanmar since 1998, just before the Ramsar Convention on Wetlands, and the current day using a Mangrove index.


Mangrove forests are unique because they can be differentiated from other terrestrial forests using remote sensing. This is done using a wetness index (MNDWI), to detect moisture, and a vegetation index (NDVI), to detect canopy. When you combine these, it’s possible to create a “Mangrove Index” (MMRI). These three indexes can be combined to create a color composite showing mangrove forest in bright green (Composite RGB = Wetness Index, Vegitation Index, Mangrove Index).

Class Project | Twitter API Analysis of COVID Hotspots


Fall 2021


This project used a twitter API, snscrape, alongside a geocoding API, nominatim, to plot the location of tweets realating to COVID across a two week period in Massachusetts.


One of the challenges was the variety of names people have for COVID, including the popular online nickname "the panini". Data aquisition was done through python, whilst mapping and analysis was accomplished using ArcPro. Results were inconclusive, and highlighted a demand for more research into the degree to which twitter analysis can harness the true sentiment of an overall population.

Research Assistant | Mapping Coastal Ecosystems Using Terrset


November 2020 - Present


Clark University is home to the developers of Terrset (formerly IDRISI), where I work as part of a team to map Mangrove, Wetland, and Pond Aquaculture in coastal equatorial regions of the world.


Maps are created using TerrSet Software to classify Landsat 5 and Landsat 8 imagery in target countries such as China, Brazil, Honduras, El Salvador, Nicaragua, and the Philippines. The work is funded through the Gordon and Betty Moore Foundation and daily tasks include using supervised clasification techniques such as Mahalanobis, and Multi-Layer Perceptron.

GIS Analyst Intern | Generation of a Topographic Basemap of Niassa National Reserve


Summer 2020


Spearheaded the creation of a comprehensive map of Niassa National Reserve for the Wildlife Conservation Societys operational planning and fieldwork. This involved ~100 hours digitizing over 5800 kilometers of roads.


A key facet of this was improving the existing road maps alongside Airfields, Bridges, and Villages. A mosaic of high-resolution sattelite imagery was used from different dates. Digitization, analysis, database management, and final map compositions were created in ArcPro.

Senior Thesis | Deforestation Trends in Belize


Fall 2020 - Present


This project employed ArcPro, alongside the Hansen et. al. deforestation dataset to researching twenty years of deforestation in Belize, and begin grappling with the multitude of drivers behind this.


Analysis was supplemented by NASA FIRMS fire point dataset, and results suggest that since 2000, although some of it has since regenerated, Belize lost 11% of its original forest cover. This research was awarded $500 for significant research through Marcia V. Szugda-Emani Memorial Environmental Scholarship.

Final Project | Mapping Coastal Flooding Vulnerability in East Anglia


Summer 2020


This project used GIS and Remote Sensing to map vulnerability to flooding across the East Anglian Region using Terrsets Climate Change and Adaptation Modeller


Challenges along the way included trying to incorporate flood defences into my models, which have a significant impact on coastal vulnerability this region. Results align with existing literature to show that the vast majority of East Anglia is incredibly vulnerable to climate change.
